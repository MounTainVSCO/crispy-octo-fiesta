I research how people form memory under uncertainty and make decisions when information is incomplete or contradictory. My recent work explores experimental design in cognitive science and how we communicate statistical evidence to the public.

Currently pursuing my MA in Psychology at Stanford University, I'm particularly interested in the intersection of cognitive psychology and science communication. My dissertation examines how uncertainty visualization affects public understanding of research findings, with implications for policy communication and health messaging.

Before graduate school, I worked as a research coordinator at the Center for Behavioral Economics, where I developed a passion for translating complex psychological research into accessible insights for diverse audiences.
