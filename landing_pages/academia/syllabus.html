Course Description

This syllabus serves as a portfolio of my academic work, research interests, and intellectual journey in cognitive science and human-computer interaction. Please feel free to explore the sections below or contact me for a full CV. My work investigates how digital interfaces shape human cognition and decision-making processes.

Required Readings / Publications

Cognitive Load in Digital Interfaces: A Framework for Ethical Design

Elena Rodriguez, Michael Chen, Sarah Thompson

CHI '24: Proceedings of the ACM Conference on Human Factors, 2024

Explores how interface complexity affects decision-making quality and proposes ethical guidelines for reducing cognitive burden in digital products.

Attention Economics and User Agency in Social Media Platforms

Elena Rodriguez

Journal of Digital Ethics, Vol. 3, No. 2, 2023

Investigates the tension between platform engagement strategies and user autonomy, with implications for regulatory frameworks.

Memory Formation in Digital Environments: Implications for Learning Design

Elena Rodriguez, David Park, Lisa Wang

Computers & Education, 2023

Examines how digital interface design influences memory consolidation and retention in educational contexts.

The Ethics of Persuasive Design: A Cognitive Science Perspective

Elena Rodriguez, Alex Johnson

ACM Transactions on Computer-Human Interaction, 2022

Analyzes the ethical implications of design patterns that leverage cognitive biases to influence user behavior.
