The Problem

Our enterprise clients were struggling with data analysis paralysis. They had tons of data but no actionable insights. Manual reporting was taking their teams 15+ hours per week, and decision-making was slow.

Market research showed 73% of similar companies wanted AI-powered insights, but existing solutions were either too complex or too generic. We saw an opportunity to build something specifically for our market.

My Role & Approach

I led the entire product development from concept to launch, working with a team of 8 engineers, 2 designers, and 1 data scientist. My responsibilities included:

  • Market research and competitive analysis
  • User interviews and requirements gathering
  • Product roadmap and feature prioritization
  • Cross-functional team coordination
  • Go-to-market strategy and launch execution

Key Decisions & Results

The biggest decision was to focus on 3 core use cases rather than trying to be everything to everyone. This allowed us to deliver exceptional value in specific areas rather than mediocre value everywhere.

Launch results exceeded all expectations. Within 6 months, the dashboard became our most-used feature and was directly responsible for closing 3 major enterprise deals worth $400K+ each.

What I'd Do Differently

I would have involved customer success earlier in the process. While the product was successful, we could have reduced onboarding time by 30% if we had better understood the change management challenges our clients faced when adopting new analytics tools.
